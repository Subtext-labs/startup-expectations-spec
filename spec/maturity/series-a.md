# Series A — stage guide

## The job of this stage
Systematize what works. You are no longer finding the answer — you are scaling
it. Every decision should reduce founder dependency and increase repeatability.
Investors are watching whether you can build a company, not just a product.

## How to read this list
**[blocking]** = required before continuing — establish these within the first 90 days.
**[conditional]** = only relevant for your specific company type — skip freely.
Many goals run in parallel. Goals 1–3 are the foundation everything else depends on.

## Phase order within Series A
**First 90 days (systemize):** goals 1–3 — establish board reporting rhythm,
lock in the repeatable GTM motion, and get the first case studies written.
These signal to your board that you're operating, not still experimenting.

**Months 3–9 (extend reach):** goals 4–6 — only add channels, international
signals, or product surface after the core motion is documented and repeatable.

**Months 6+ (institutional readiness):** goals 7–13 — compliance, security
maturity, and insurance. These are gating requirements for enterprise deals and
Series B diligence, not just nice-to-haves.

**Ongoing (team scale):** goals 14–19 — remote hiring, immigration, and advisor
governance as your team grows beyond the founding core.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Common traps at this stage**

- **Hiring executives before processes exist** — a VP Sales with no playbook, no CRM, and no defined ICP has nothing to manage. Document the motion first.
- **International before domestic** — opening a London office before your home market motion is repeatable just doubles operational complexity with no new signal.
- **Adding product surface before the core is defensible** — a second product line that splits engineering attention often kills the first one.
- **Board reporting as performance** — updates that only show good news train your board to be useless in a crisis. Use reporting to surface real blockers.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="success" %}
**Exit signals: when you're ready for Series B**

- Sales cycle is documented, with a playbook a non-founder can execute
- Gross margin is understood, measured, and trending in the right direction
- Key executive roles filled — at minimum VP/Head of Sales, Engineering, and Product
- You can describe the Series B thesis in one sentence: "We're scaling X motion into Y market, which unlocks Z"
{% endhint %}
