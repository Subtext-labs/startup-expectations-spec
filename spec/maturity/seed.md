# Seed — stage guide

## The job of this stage
Get to first repeatable signal — paying users, measurable retention, or a
clearly de-risked thesis. The question investors are asking: can you acquire
and keep customers without heroics?

## How to read this list
**[blocking]** = required before goals listed after it.
**[conditional]** = only relevant in specific situations — skip freely.
The list mixes blocking goals with parallel tracks. Don't work through it
linearly — run the customer track and the housekeeping track simultaneously.

## Phase order within seed
**First 30 days post-raise (housekeeping):** goals 1–5 — get cap table, burn
model, and milestone targets locked before spending the money.

**Months 1–4 (customer traction):** goals 6–9 — get paying users and instrument
what they do. This is the only evidence that matters for Series A.

**Months 3–6 (scaling attempts):** goals 10–14 — only after you have paying
users. Reliability, contracts, and community are for companies with customers.

**Months 6+ (Series A prep):** goals 15–21 — legal, security, and team
infrastructure that Series A investors will check in diligence.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Common traps at this stage**

- **Fundraising before customers** — raising seed before you have paying users means you're selling a story, not traction. You'll raise at a lower valuation and with less leverage.
- **Hiring sales before founders can sell** — if you can't close deals yourself, a sales hire won't either. Founders must own the first 5–10 closes.
- **Too many design partners** — 4+ design partners with different needs will fracture your roadmap. One or two with aligned use cases is better.
- **Confusing activity with traction** — meetings, signups, and LOIs are not traction. Retention and payment are.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="success" %}
**Exit signals: when you're ready for Series A**

- Named customers paying real money with measurable retention or repeat usage
- At least one customer acquisition path that works without founder involvement
- Team of 5–12 with clear ownership across eng, product, and customer-facing roles
- You can articulate why the next $X million makes the motion faster, not just bigger
{% endhint %}
