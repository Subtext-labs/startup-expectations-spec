# Raising a seed round

**Stage:** Seed · **Category:** Funding & runway

> Get seed funding with a clear story, real traction signals, and a believable plan for the money.

---

## What you are aiming for

A closed round with signed documents, funds wired, and a reporting cadence established with new investors. The goal is not to raise at the highest valuation — it is to raise from investors who understand your space, with terms and a timeline that give you 18+ months of runway to reach the next milestone.

## What to focus on

Put clarity for investors first — traction, milestones, and how you will spend the round. Internal engineering detail is secondary. Investors are deciding whether they believe your thesis and your team, not auditing your code.

- **Pipeline discipline** — track every investor conversation; know the stage of each one
- **Tight data room** — cap table, financials, and key metrics ready before diligence starts
- **The ask** — a specific amount, a clear use of funds, and a story for why this round gets you to the next milestone

## Deprioritize for now

- Perfecting slide design over narrative clarity
- Chasing every inbound intro regardless of fit
- Negotiating every term before you have a lead investor

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No process** | No deck, no investor outreach, no fundraising plan |
| **L1 — Informal outreach** | Deck exists, warm intro meetings happening, nothing tracked |
| **L2 — Active process** | Pipeline tracked, data room ready, clear ask, in diligence conversations |
| **L3 — Round closed** | Signed docs, funds wired, cap table updated, first investor update sent |

## Team impact

Engineering and people expectations still apply during a raise. The risk is that fundraising consumes founder attention and slows product and customer work. Protect at least 50% of non-fundraising time — a stalled product narrative hurts the raise anyway.
