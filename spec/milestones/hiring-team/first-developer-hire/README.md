# Hiring your first developer

**Stage:** Pre-seed · **Category:** Team & hiring

> Make your first engineering hire when you know what they will own and how they will ramp on a tiny team.

---

## What you are aiming for

An engineering hire who is shipping independently within 60 days — not a pair-programming dependency on the founders. The signal that this milestone is done is not the offer signed; it is the first feature or fix shipped without founder involvement.

## What to focus on

- **Role clarity first** — define what this person will own that founders cannot or should not own; write the job description before sourcing
- **Level fit** — a senior hire on a 3-person team needs to be comfortable with ambiguity and zero process; a mid-level hire needs clear scope and a mentor
- **Interview signal** — use a work sample or short take-home that mirrors real work; abstract puzzles do not predict early-stage fit
- **First weeks plan** — write a 30-day onboarding plan before the offer is signed; the first task should be something they can own end-to-end
- **Compensation at stage** — benchmark against early-stage norms, not Series B bands; over-titling or over-paying creates problems at the next hire

## Deprioritize for now

- Elaborate interview loops designed for larger teams
- Comprehensive benefits packages before you have HR infrastructure
- Hiring to fill a headcount number rather than a specific ownership gap

## Quality levels

| Level | What it looks like |
|-------|--------------------|
| **L0 — No hire** | Founders building or outsourcing; no role defined |
| **L1 — Searching** | Job description written, sourcing started, bar not yet calibrated |
| **L2 — Hired** | Offer signed, 30-day onboarding plan written, access and tooling ready |
| **L3 — Productive** | Hire shipping independently within 60 days; 1:1s running; early feedback loop in place |

## Team impact

The first developer hire sets the culture and bar for every subsequent hire. Be explicit about what "good" looks like in this role — if you are not, you will either under-hire (and the person drowns) or over-hire (and the person is bored and leaves). The founders remain the culture carriers; this hire amplifies them.
